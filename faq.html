MotionCue Help & FAQ

Short answers to the questions we hear most.

MotionCue is built to be simple: press play and follow the figure. If something isn't behaving the way you expect, the answer is usually here.

Reminders & notifications

I'm not getting reminders

Reminders are delivered through your device's notifications, so they need permission to appear. Check, in order:

  • Notifications are allowed. Open the iPhone Settings app > MotionCue > Notifications, and make sure Allow Notifications is on, along with Lock Screen, Notification Center, and Banners.
  • You haven't muted them with a Focus. A Focus or Do Not Disturb (including Sleep and a scheduled Focus) will silence reminders. Add MotionCue as an allowed app, or check that a Focus isn't running when you expect a reminder.
  • Sound is on. For an audible chime, set the reminder style to "Sound & vibration" in MotionCue > Settings, and take the phone off silent. On silent, the reminder still appears and vibrates; it just won't play a sound.
  • Your schedule allows it. In MotionCue > Settings, confirm reminders are on, the day is one of your active days, and the current time is inside your waking window (outside quiet hours).

Reminders follow the cadence you set and roll forward from your last finished session, so you won't be nudged right after you've already stretched.

I don't feel reminders on my Apple Watch

This is usually how iPhone and Apple Watch are designed to work together, not a problem with the app:

  • iPhone sends an alert to your Apple Watch only when your phone is locked or unattended and the watch is on your wrist and unlocked. While you're actively using the phone, the alert stays on the phone.
  • To feel a reminder on your wrist: lock the phone, keep the watch on, and wait for the next scheduled time.
  • Check the Watch app on your iPhone > Notifications to confirm MotionCue is set to mirror your iPhone, and that the watch isn't in its own Do Not Disturb or Theater Mode.

Sessions & stretches

Press the play button on Home to run your whole routine hands-free; MotionCue advances through each stretch and tells you when to switch sides. To try a single stretch on its own, tap it in the Library and use "Try stretch," or tap a stretch on Home. Starting a full session resets your reminder interval.

An optional soft chime and haptic can mark when it's time to switch sides mid-stretch, and a cue plays when each stretch finishes, useful when you can't watch the screen. Turn these on under Settings > "Chime and haptics."

Water

Use the − and + buttons on the Home water row to add or remove water (one tap is one serving, sized to your unit). Turn on "Sync with Apple Health" in Settings to also save water to Health and include water you've logged in other apps in your daily total. It's optional; the app works fully without it.

Themes & music

Choose a look under Settings > appearance. An optional, gentle session soundtrack can play while you stretch; turn "Session music" on in Settings. It mixes under your own audio and respects your silent switch.

Apple Watch

MotionCue runs on both iPhone and Apple Watch and is included with your subscription, there's nothing extra to buy. Subscribe on your iPhone; your subscription, preferences, sessions, and streaks sync between your own devices over Apple's encrypted device-to-device channel.

Circle

Circle is an optional way to keep a small, encouraging group with people you invite, so a quiet daily habit has a little company. It stays off until you create or join a Circle.

Starting or joining a Circle

On the Circle tab, tap New Circle to start one (name it something like "Family" or "Work crew"), then tap Share invite link and send it to the people you want in. To join someone else's, open their invite link or tap Join with a link. You can be in several Circles at once, and joining one never touches the others.

What my Circle can see

Only three things, and only the people in that Circle see them: the display name you choose for Circle, your current streak and whether you've stretched today, and the cheers you send and receive. Never the specific stretches you did, your reminder schedule, or your hydration. It's stored in your own iCloud and shared through Apple's private sharing; it never passes through our servers and we never receive a copy. Using Circle requires being signed in to iCloud.

Cheers

Tap someone in your Circle to send a cheer (Love it, Proud of you, or Nice work). They feel a soft buzz on their wrist and see a small bloom on their card. You can leave any Circle at any time, which removes your presence from it.

Do people I invite have to pay?

When someone you invite joins your Circle, MotionCue opens for them without the upfront paywall, so a friend you bring in can start moving right away.

Subscription

MotionCue is one simple subscription that includes everything, every stretch, both devices, all themes, and any future additions. There is no separate "Pro" tier and nothing is locked behind a second purchase.

  • Manage or cancel: open the App Store, tap your account photo, tap Subscriptions, then select MotionCue. Cancelling stops the next renewal; you keep access until the current period ends.
  • Restore: if you reinstall or switch devices, tap "Restore Purchases" on the subscription screen to recover an active subscription tied to your Apple ID.

About the stretches

Every stretch is drawn from a published health authority (such as Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, Harvard Health, and the APTA), with the source cited in the app. MotionCue is a general wellness app, not a medical device or a substitute for professional medical advice. If a movement causes pain, stop and consult a clinician.
